About

CaseMaster is a highly practical, interactive and advanced case-based problem solving program. The objective of the course is to support you in building a strong problem solving muscle that would enable you to greatly increase the efficiency and quality of your problem solving abilities no matter your background or line work.

During the program, we will present you with a series of business problems (e.g., estimating the market size for a specific product or service, designing a market entry strategy, developing a growth strategy for a company, evaluating potential acquisition targets, developing a new government policy), and share with you tools and methodologies that you can use to analyze those business problems like a top tier management consultant would, along with examples on how these tools can be applied successfully.

For each business problem, we will provide you with real-life case studies, and give you opportunity to roll-up your sleeves and solve them in break-out rooms with other participants. After solving the case in your breakout rooms, we will reconvene for a comprehensive debriefing to reflect on your experience solving the case and addressing any questions you may have.

Each module will be led by an experienced management consultant who is an Alumnus / Alumnae from a top tier consulting firm like McKinsey & Company, The Boston Consulting Group, or Bain & Company. The program has eight modules of 2 hours each, meaning you will go through the above described experience eight times. Repeating this cycle so many times, across so many different topics, with many different groups, and facilitated by experienced coaches, will significantly improve your ability to successfully solve problems in your day-to-day careers.

Typical Module Agenda

  • We introduce a specific type of business challenge or framework (e.g., "lean management" or market sizing"), explain how it is relevant, as well as common approaches to think about this topic in a realistic day-to-day business scenario, showcasing several examples.

  • Open Q&A and discussion with faculty on case or any other questions participants might have on the topic.

Dr. Annas Abedin

Annas is a co-founder and managing director of Navital Global, a global community of management consulting professionals (current and former, full-time and freelance) that aims to be the most effective and impactful career development platform globally for professionals who have a background in management consulting. Prior to co-founding Navital, he co-founded Makeen Advisors (a MENA focused network of independent consulting professionals), the Qimam Fellowship, Namshi, and the Middle East chapter of e-commerce company Rocket Internet. He was also an Engagement Manager with McKinsey & Company.

Cyrille Alheritiere

Cyrille spent 14 years at UBS as Chief of Staff, then COO for UBS’s Middle Eastern and Latin American sizable offshore businesses. In the second half of his career, he headed Brazil Switzerland and then UBS’s LatAm Geneva business. Prior to joining UBS, Cyrille was a Senior Associate at McKinsey & Company, covering the Financial Industry. In his early career he led IT support and development for the trading operations of Dresdner Kleinwort Wasserstein in Singapore. He holds a PhD in Process Systems Engineering from the University College London, and a Master of Science Chemical Engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology in Atlanta Georgia.
